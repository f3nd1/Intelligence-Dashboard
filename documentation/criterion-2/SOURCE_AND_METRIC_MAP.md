# Criterion 2 Source and Metric Map

## 2.1.1 Staff Selection and Management

Primary candidates:

- Employee
- Job Requisition
- Job Applicant
- Interview Feedback
- Employee Onboarding
- Appraisal
- Employee Separation
- Exit Interview

Live checks include active staff, requisitions, applicants, selection outcomes,
interviews, onboarding, appraisal, separation and exit records.

## 2.1.2 Staff Training and Development

Primary candidates:

- Training Event
- Training Needs Analysis
- Training Program
- Training Result
- Training Feedback
- Training Sponsorship Application
- Appraisal
- Employee

Live checks include training events, needs, programmes, results, feedback and
sponsorship. Employee-level training coverage remains unsupported until the
attendance relationship is confirmed.

## 2.2.1 Internal and External Communication

Primary candidates:

- Stakeholder Registry
- Stakeholder Engagement Strategy
- Material Vetting Form
- Essential Information

Live checks include communication materials, approvals, stakeholder coverage,
channels, frequency and review dates.

## 2.3.1 Data and Information Management

Primary candidates:

- Document Control
- Quality Performance Outcomes
- Quality Meeting
- Quality Action

The policy requires controlled data collection, storage, security, accuracy,
consent, quality review and integrated reporting.

## 2.3.2 Knowledge Management

Primary candidates:

- Document Control
- Quality Meeting
- Quality Action

Live checks include current, obsolete, archived and overdue controlled
documents. Record-disposal completion remains unsupported until retention and
disposal fields are confirmed.

## 2.4.1 Feedback Management

Primary candidates:

- Survey Management
- Survey Tracking
- HD Ticket / Helpdesk Ticket / Issue
- Employee Grievance
- Quality Action

Live checks include open and closed feedback, SLA failure, grievances and
feedback-related Quality Actions.

## 2.4.2 and 2.4.3 Satisfaction Surveys

Survey Management and Survey Tracking are resolved independently for student
and staff surveys. The average rating can be calculated when an approved rating
or score field exists. Coverage percentages require a confirmed eligible
population and survey-cycle denominator.
